# Cold Outreach Email Templates (15)

---

## Email 1 — The "Quick Question" (Highest Reply Rate)

**Subject:** quick question

Hey {{name}},

Noticed you're running {{company}} — looks like you're doing {{specific observation}}.

Quick question: how are you currently handling {{pain point}}?

— {{your name}}

✅ WHY IT WORKS:
- 2 sentences = easy to reply to
- Specific observation shows you actually researched them
- Ends with one open question, not a pitch
- No links, no attachments, no friction

---

## Email 2 — The "Compliment + Problem" (B2B SaaS)

**Subject:** {{company}}'s {{specific thing}}

Hey {{name}},

Loved your recent {{blog post / tweet / product launch}} about {{topic}}.

One thing I noticed — a lot of companies doing {{what they do}} still struggle with {{pain point}}. We built {{product}} specifically to fix that.

Worth a 15-min call this week?

{{your name}}

✅ WHY IT WORKS:
- Opens with genuine compliment (not flattery)
- Bridges from compliment to pain naturally
- Single CTA with low commitment (15 min only)

## Email 3 — The "Competitor Customer" (High Conversion)

**Subject:** switching from {{competitor}}?

Hey {{name}},

Saw you're using {{competitor}} — a lot of their customers have been reaching out to us lately after {{recent competitor problem / price increase / outage}}.

We built {{product}} to do exactly what {{competitor}} does, but {{key differentiator}}.

Happy to show you a quick demo — no pressure.

{{your name}}

✅ WHY IT WORKS:
- Timely (references a real event)
- No trash-talking competitor, just factual
- "No pressure" reduces friction at the CTA

## Email 4 — The "Mutual Connection" (Warm Intro Feel)

**Subject:** {{mutual name}} suggested I reach out

Hey {{name}},

{{mutual connection}} mentioned you're working on {{problem area}} and thought we should chat.

We help {{target customer}} with {{specific outcome}} — {{mutual name}} saw {{result we got them}}.

Would love to show you what we built. Do you have 20 mins this week?

{{your name}}

✅ WHY IT WORKS:
- Social proof baked into sentence 1
- Specific result mentioned (not vague "we help companies grow")
- Feels warm even if barely warm

## Email 5 — The "Trigger Event" (Best Timing)

**Subject:** congrats on the funding

Hey {{name}},

Congrats on the {{Series A / launch / new hire announcement}} — exciting milestone.

Companies at your stage usually start running into {{specific pain point}} around this time. We've helped {{similar company}} solve exactly that.

Worth a quick chat?

{{your name}}

✅ WHY IT WORKS:
- Triggered by a real event (not random)
- Shows you follow their company
- Pain point is timed perfectly to their growth stage

## Email 6 — The "One-Liner" (Ultra Short)

**Subject:** {{product}} for {{company}}

Hey {{name}} — would it be helpful if {{specific outcome your product delivers}}?

{{your name}}

✅ WHY IT WORKS:
- Entire email is one sentence
- Hard to not answer a direct yes/no question
- Works best for warm-ish leads who've heard of you

## Email 7 — The "Case Study" (Proof-First)

**Subject:** how {{similar company}} got {{result}}

Hey {{name}},

Quick story — {{similar company}} was struggling with {{pain point}}.

They tried {{product}} and within {{timeframe}} they {{specific measurable result}}.

Think it could work for {{their company}} too. Want me to send over the details?

{{your name}}

✅ WHY IT WORKS:
- Story format is more readable than features
- Specific result builds credibility immediately
- "Want me to send details?" is lower friction than "book a demo"

## Email 9 — The "Permission Ask" (Non-Pushy)

**Subject:** okay to send something over?

Hey {{name}},

I have an idea for how {{company}} could {{specific benefit}}.

Okay if I send it over?

{{your name}}

✅ WHY IT WORKS:
- Asking permission gets higher response than sending unsolicited pitch
- Creates curiosity without revealing the idea
- Only 2 sentences — impossible to ignore

**Subject:** {{X}}% of {{company type}} have this problem

Hey {{name}},

- Urgency comes from the problem, not from you

## Email 13 — The "Job Posting Intel" (Intent Signal)

**Subject:** saw you're hiring a {{role}}

Hey {{name}},

Noticed {{company}} is hiring a {{role}} — usually means you're scaling {{specific function}}.

A lot of companies at that stage use {{product}} to handle {{related task}} before making that hire (or instead of it).

Would that be useful to look at?

{{your name}}

✅ WHY IT WORKS:
- Job postings are public intent signals
- Frames product as "before you spend on headcount"
- Relevant timing = high open and reply rate

## Email 14 — The "Breakup" (Re-engage Gone Cold)

**Subject:** closing your file

Hey {{name}},

I've reached out a few times and haven't heard back — totally understand if the timing isn't right.

I'll close your file for now. If {{pain point}} ever becomes a priority, just reply and I'll pick it back up.

{{your name}}

✅ WHY IT WORKS:
- "Closing your file" triggers FOMO and reply
- No guilt, no pressure — gives them an out
- Often generates replies from people who were just busy

## Email 15 — The "Referral Ask" (When They Say No)

**Subject:** re: {{original email subject}}

Hey {{name}},

Totally understand if it's not the right fit.

Quick ask — is there anyone else at {{company}} or in your network who might find {{product}} useful? Happy to make it worth your while.

{{your name}}

✅ WHY IT WORKS:
- Turns a no into a referral opportunity
- Specific ask ("anyone in your network") is easy to answer
- No hard feelings tone keeps relationship warm
